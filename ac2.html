Pronouns


Overview of Grammar topic:

  • Reflexive pronouns (myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves) are used when the subject and object refer to the same person/thing.

  • Interrogative pronouns (who, what, which, whose, why, how) are used to ask questions and request information.

  • Relative pronouns (who, whom, whose, which, that) introduce a relative clause that modifies or provides extra information about a noun or noun phrase.

Pronoun type Usage Example
Reflexive pronouns Use reflexive pronouns when the subject and object refer to the same person or thing. myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves
Interrogative pronouns Use interrogative pronouns to ask questions and request specific information. who, what, which, whose, why
Relative pronouns Relative pronouns introduce a clause that modifies or describes a noun or pronoun. who, whom, whose, which, that
